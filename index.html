Pomodoro Timer
The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a timer to break work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. This technique helps improve focus, productivity, and mental clarity by encouraging frequent breaks and reducing burnout.

Steps to Use the Pomodoro Technique

  1. Choose a Task

    Select a task or project you want to work on.

  2. Set the Timer

    Set a timer for 25 minutes (this is one "Pomodoro"). Commit to working on the task without distractions during this time.

  3. Work on the Task

    Focus entirely on the task until the timer rings. Avoid multitasking or checking emails, messages, or social media.

  4. Take a Short Break

    When the timer goes off, take a 5-minute break. Use this time to relax, stretch, or grab a drink.

  5. Repeat the Process

    After completing four Pomodoros, take a longer break (15â€“30 minutes). This helps recharge your energy and maintain productivity.

  6. Track Progress

    Keep a record of completed Pomodoros to track your productivity and accomplishments.

Benefits of the Pomodoro Technique

  • Improves focus and concentration.
  • Reduces procrastination and mental fatigue.
  • Encourages a sense of accomplishment.
  • Helps manage time effectively.
